# Aleph Blog Writing Guide & SEO Playbook

## Blog Post Template Structure

Every post follows this skeleton:

1. **Meta tags** (title, description, OG, Twitter, canonical, JSON-LD)
2. **Byline**: `By James Chang` + date + read time
3. **TL;DR box** (top, 3-5 bullets, under 100 words)
4. **Introduction** (2-3 paragraphs, primary keyword in first 100 words, open with a scenario)
5. **Body** (3-5 H2 sections, each with at least one non-paragraph element)
6. **Mid-post CTA** (after H2 #2 or #3)
7. **Key Takeaways box** (bottom, action-oriented)
8. **End CTA** (stronger ask)
9. **Author bio**
10. **Related posts** (2-3 links)

## AI Attribution

- **Per-post byline**: `By James Chang, Founder of Aleph` (no per-post AI mention)
- **Site-level disclosure** (on About page): "Our posts are written with AI assistance. Every post is reviewed for accuracy and reflects direct experience with US import compliance."
- Rationale: James has genuine domain expertise, reviews all content. Google's E-E-A-T framework rewards expert-reviewed content regardless of production method.

## Tone

- **Target**: "Smart friend who happens to be an expert"
- Use contractions (you're, it's, don't)
- Open with scenarios, not definitions
- Use "you" and "your" liberally
- Acknowledge regulatory absurdity with dry humor
- Use concrete numbers and specifics
- No jargon without context, no legalese, no marketing fluff
- No exclamation points (1 per post max)
