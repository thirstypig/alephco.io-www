The Challenge

Food importing is heavily regulated

FSVP is mandatory

Every food importer must have a Foreign Supplier Verification Program under FDA’s FSMA rules. No FSVP means your products can be detained at the border.

Supplier verification is ongoing

You must evaluate and re-evaluate each foreign supplier’s food safety performance. One supplier across multiple products means multiple verification records to maintain.

FDA inspections are unannounced

FDA can request your FSVP records at any time. If you can’t produce organized documentation quickly, you face import alerts, warning letters, or product detention.

The Solution

Your FSVP program, handled

Aleph gives food importers a structured system for managing FSVP documentation, supplier verification, and ongoing compliance — without needing a compliance team.

Supplier profiles

Create and maintain detailed profiles for every foreign supplier. Track certifications, audit history, and verification activities in one place.

Hazard analysis

Complete guided hazard analysis questionnaires for each food product. Identify known hazards and document the controls your supplier has in place.

Corrective actions

When issues arise, document corrective actions and track resolution. Maintain a clear record of how you addressed each problem for FDA review.
